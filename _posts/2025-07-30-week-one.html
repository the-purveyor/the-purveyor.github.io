Graphs

. //* Bar graph

>     if Region=="South Asia",                                 ///
>     over(CountryCode, sort(Constant_GDP2022) descending     ///

.

file ./Stata/fig1-bar-gdp.svg saved as SVG format

The following bar chart is added as a result.

The picture does not appear very clear. We will therefore express these values in units of billions of USD.

. //* Generate a variable that expresses the GDP in bn. USD
. gen gdp2022bn = Constant_GDP2022/1000000000
(10 missing values generated)

.     
. //* Bar graph of `gdp202bn` for UMIC.
. graph bar (asis) gdp2022bn                                     ///
>     if Region=="South Asia",                                 ///
>     over(CountryCode, sort(gdp2022bn) descending             ///
>     label(angle(ninety) labsize(vsmall)))

.     
. graph export "./Stata/fig2-bar-gdpbn.svg",                     ///
>     as(svg) width(500) replace
file ./Stata/fig2-bar-gdpbn.svg saved as SVG format

The following bar chart is generated.

Scale variations are significant across the UMICs. To bring parity in comparison we calculate the natural logarithm of the GDP-Constant-prices series.

. //* Generate natural log of GDP-Constant-prices
. gen lngdp22 = ln(Constant_GDP2022)
(10 missing values generated)

We generate the bar graph of the natural log of GDP in constant prices below.

. //* Bar graph of `lngdp22`
. graph bar (asis) lngdp22                                     ///
>     if Region=="South Asia",                                 ///
>     over(CountryCode, sort(Constant_GDP2022) descending     ///
>     label(angle(ninety) labsize(vsmall)))

.         
. graph export "./Stata/fig3-bar-gdppc.svg", as(svg) width(500) replace    
file ./Stata/fig3-bar-gdppc.svg saved as SVG format
